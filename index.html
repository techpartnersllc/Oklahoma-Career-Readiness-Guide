Oklahoma Career Readiness Resource Guide

A practical index of literacy, ESL, GED/HiSET, adult education, and workforce-readiness resources in Tulsa, Oklahoma City, and across Oklahoma — built for people who may need to solve foundational barriers before pursuing a career pathway.

Adult Literacy ESL Support GED / HiSET Workforce Readiness Community Referrals

This guide is designed as a practical resource on the Tech Partners website. Its goal is to make foundational support easier to find, easier to share, and easier to use for individuals and community partners.

Overview: This guide is designed to come before the Oklahoma Career Pathways Guide. It focuses on readiness first — helping people address literacy, language, and educational barriers so they can return to career planning when they are ready.
Part 1

Foundation Resources by Need

Who this guide is for

Adults who want to work, train, or pursue a better future, but may first need support with reading, writing, English language development, high school completion, or basic educational confidence.

Community partners resource

Case managers, churches, schools, nonprofits, and workforce programs can use this guide as a referral tool when someone is not ready to move directly into a career pathway or job opportunity.

Why it fits Tech Partners

This extends Tech Partners' workforce-development mission by identifying the first barrier to solve, then helping people reconnect to future pathway planning once readiness improves.

Common Readiness Barriers

  • Adult literacy needs — reading, writing, and comprehension support.
  • English language learning — ESL classes, conversation support, citizenship prep.
  • High school equivalency — GED or HiSET preparation and testing pathways.
  • General readiness support — confidence, digital literacy, adult education navigation, and job-readiness foundations.

GED / HiSET Programs

Where adults can start

  • Tulsa Community College Workforce & Continuing Education offers GED test preparation and continuing education pathways that can bridge readiness and future occupational training.
  • Community Literacy Centers includes high school equivalency support among its adult learning class offerings in Oklahoma City.

Part 6

Finding Your On-Ramp

Wherever someone is starting — rebuilding confidence, learning English, finishing high school equivalency, or preparing for work — there is a sequence that can move them toward opportunity.

1. Intake Identify goals, urgency, and the most immediate barrier.
2. Readiness Assessment Determine whether the person is ready now or needs support first.
3. Referral Path Connect them to literacy, ESL, GED, or adult education resources.
4. Reassessment Check progress and revisit options after foundational gains.
5. Career Pathways Return the person to pathway selection, training, and employer connection.

How to use this guide

This guide can stand on its own as a Tech Partners public resource today, while also supporting future conversations about a stronger community navigation model that helps more adults move from “not ready yet” to “ready now.”
